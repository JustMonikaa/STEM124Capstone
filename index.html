Phase 1: Conceptualization & Gap Analysis

Weeks 1-3. The objective is to move from a general idea to a specific, patentable novelty. We skip the theory and jump straight into community problem solving.

WEEK 1

Novelty Check

Search Google Patents and IPOPHIL. If your idea exists, you must pivot to a "Specific Improvement".

WEEK 2

Technical Schematic

Draw the System Architecture. What are the inputs? What is the mechanism?

WEEK 3

The Proposal Defense

Present to a panel of STEM teachers. They will check for Feasibility.

Phase 2: Development & Prototyping

Weeks 4-7. This is the "Lab" phase. You are no longer students; you are engineers.

Logistics & Sourcing (Week 4)

Purchase materials. For electronics: Shopee/Lazada. For mechanical: Local hardware stores.

The "Build-Fail-Fix" Cycle (Weeks 5-6)

Assemble the prototype. Document every error code or structural break.

Phase 3: Testing & Statistical Interpretation

Weeks 8-10. Prove your product works better than the status quo using data.

Experimental Design (Week 8)

Run Trial 1, 2, and 3. Never rely on a single test. Ensure you have a Control Group.

Data Visualization

Phase 4: Defense & Submission

Weeks 11-13. Master your data, polish your slides, and prepare for the defense.
