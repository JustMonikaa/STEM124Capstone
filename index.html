Phase 1: Conceptualization & Gap Analysis

Weeks 1-3. The objective is to move from a general idea to a specific, patentable novelty. We skip the theory and jump straight into community problem solving.

WEEK 1

Novelty Check

Search Google Patents and IPOPHIL. If your idea exists, you must pivot to a "Specific Improvement" (e.g., lower cost using local materials).

WEEK 2

Technical Schematic

Draw the System Architecture. What are the inputs? What is the mechanism? What is the measurable output?

WEEK 3

The Proposal Defense

Present to a panel of STEM teachers. They will check for Feasibility. If they say it's too expensive or too long, you MUST pivot now.

Phase 1 Deliverables (Checklist)

  • Gap Analysis Matrix (Existing Tech vs. Your Tech)
  • Finalized Research Title (Invention Name)
  • Approved Chapter 1-3 Draft (Methodology Focus)

Phase 2: Development & Prototyping

Weeks 4-7. This is the "Lab" phase. You are no longer students; you are engineers. Expect Version 1.0 to fail. Failure is data.

Logistics & Sourcing (Week 4)

Purchase materials. For electronics: Shopee/Lazada (expect 3-5 days delivery). For mechanical: Local hardware stores. Pro Tip: Buy 20% more materials than you need for "safety stock".

The "Build-Fail-Fix" Cycle (Weeks 5-6)

Assemble the prototype. If it's a software app, finish the Core Logic. If it's a product, finish the Mechanical Framework. Document every error code or structural break.

The Engineering Logbook

Your logbook is your legal proof of work. It should include:

  • Daily entry of work done
  • Annotated photos of the assembly process
  • Signed safety checklist for lab use
  • Bill of Materials (Finalized Costs)

Phase 3: Testing & Statistical Interpretation

Weeks 8-10. This is where your invention becomes "Science". You must prove your product works better than the status quo using data.

Experimental Design (Week 8)

Run Trial 1, 2, and 3. Never rely on a single test. Ensure you have a Control Group (the existing way of doing things) and an Experimental Group (your invention).

Example:
Control: Commercial Fertilizer Growth Rate
Experimental: Your Bio-Fertilizer Growth Rate

Statistical Decision (Week 9-10)

Transfer your logbook numbers to Excel. Use the Stats Engine in this app to pick the right test. Write Chapter 4 (Results & Discussion).

Phase 4: Defense & Submission

Weeks 11-13. The final stretch. Master your data, polish your slides, and prepare for the hardest 30 minutes of your SHS life.

Week 11: Final Paper

Finish Chapter 5 (Recommendations). Complete the Appendices (Photos, Letters, Original Data). Proofread for APA 7th Edition format.

Week 12: Mock Defense

Present to your peers. They must act like "Mean Panelists". Practice answering: "Why is this better than what we already have?"

Week 13: Final Board

Final defense. 10 minutes presentation, 20 minutes Q&A. Submit the hardbound manuscript following panel revisions.

Anticipated Panel Questions (Top 3)

1. "What is your statistical significance?"

Answer with your p-value and what it means for your hypothesis.

2. "How cost-effective is your design for mass production?"

Refer to your Bill of Materials and compare unit cost with commercial competitors.

Expert Statistical Decision Engine

1. What is the nature of your Dependent Variable (DV)?

2. How many Independent Variables (IV) or groups?

3. Are the samples Independent or Paired?
